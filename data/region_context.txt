ARIA OPERATIONAL CONTEXT — INDIA DISASTER RESPONSE

PRIMARY JURISDICTION: Republic of India
COORDINATING AUTHORITY: National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)
FIELD AUTHORITY: State Disaster Management Authorities (SDMAs)
RESPONSE FORCE: National Disaster Response Force (NDRF), State Disaster Response Force (SDRF)

HIGH-RISK ZONES:
- Coastal Odisha & Andhra Pradesh: cyclone corridor (Bay of Bengal), Oct–May season
- Northeast India & Himalayan foothills: landslides, cloudbursts (Jun–Sep)
- Godavari, Krishna, Brahmaputra basins: annual flooding (Jul–Sep)
- Jammu & Kashmir (NH44 corridor): landslides, road cuts (monsoon + winter)
- Maharashtra industrial belt: chemical/industrial incidents (year-round)

CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE:
- National Highway network (NH44, NH16, NH48 are priority corridors)
- Major river systems: Godavari, Krishna, Mahanadi, Brahmaputra, Ganga
- Coastal ports: Paradip, Visakhapatnam, Chennai, Mumbai

NDRF BATTALION LOCATIONS (key bases):
- Patna, Kolkata, Mundali (Odisha), Vijayawada, Pune, Nagpur, Jammu, Guwahati, Gandhinagar, Varanasi

RESPONSE DOCTRINE:
1. Life safety is the primary objective — search and rescue before property protection
2. Vulnerable populations (elderly, children, disabled) are priority evacuation targets
3. Medical units should be pre-positioned within 30 minutes of critical incidents
4. Helicopters are reserved for inaccessible areas and mass casualty evacuation
5. Inter-agency coordination is mandatory for multi-district incidents
6. NDRF teams should not be split — deploy full teams to maximize effectiveness

COMMUNICATION CHANNELS:
- IWIN (India Disaster Resource Network) for resource tracking
- NDMA control room: 1078
- State EOC coordination required for all severity ≥ 70 incidents
